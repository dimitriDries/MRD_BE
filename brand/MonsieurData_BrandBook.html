MonsieurData

Identity guidelines for MonsieurData, the digital analytics practice of Dimitri Dries, digital analytics developer. Use this document as the single source of truth for every future design: web, decks, documents, social.

01 · Brand idea

The gentleman & the slash.

The brand pairs two ideas: the monsieur (classic, trustworthy, precise, a touch of wit) and the slash (sharp, modern, decisive). Everything designed for MonsieurData should balance those two: calm, craftsmanlike surfaces cut by one confident accent. Professional first, edge second, never gimmicky.

02 · Logo

Logo usage.

The primary mark is the gentleman silhouette crossed by the red slash. The thumbnail (cropped) version is for favicons, avatars and small spaces.

Do
  • Give the logo clear space equal to the height of the slash on all sides.
  • Use the thumbnail mark below 48px.
  • Place on Paper (#FAF8F4), white, or Ink (#16120F) backgrounds only.
Don't
  • Recolour the slash or the silhouette.
  • Rotate, outline, shadow or distort the mark.
  • Place the logo on busy photos or mid-tone backgrounds.

05 · The slash motif

One angle, everywhere.

The slash from the logo is the brand's signature graphic element. It always leans at 16° from vertical (CSS: skewX(-16deg)). It appears as section accents, list markers, the "/" in labels, and the clipped corner of primary buttons.

Book a free intro call / EYEBROW LABEL
Do
  • Use at most one large slash per screen/page.
  • Keep the 16° angle exactly: consistency is the brand.
  • Use reduced opacity (25–45%) for layered/background slashes.
Don't
  • Mirror the slash (it always leans right-to-left, top-right high).
  • Use other diagonal angles or zig-zags.
  • Combine with gradients, glows or 3D effects.

06 · Voice & tone

Sound like the monsieur.

Confident, plain-spoken, lightly witty. Write like a senior developer who has nothing to prove: short sentences, concrete claims, no buzzwords, no exclamation marks. Dry humour is welcome in small doses (one wink per page, maximum).

Say
  • "Reporting people actually use."
  • "Clear scope, no lock-in."
  • "If nobody opens it, it doesn't exist."
Avoid
  • "Unlock the power of data-driven synergies!"
  • "World-class, cutting-edge solutions."
  • Anything you wouldn't say out loud to a client.
